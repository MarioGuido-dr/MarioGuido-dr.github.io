I am a Doctoral Researcher and Graduate Lecturer in Law at University College London (UCL), specialising in criminal justice, prison systems, labour law, and the intersection of law and technology. My research focuses on prison labour systems, with particular attention to the role of privatisation, labour rights, and questions of fairness, rehabilitation, and reintegration.

I hold an LLB (First Class Honours) from the University of Reading and an LLM from UCL. I am currently completing my PhD at UCL, where my research examines the structure and regulation of private prison labour systems and explores pathways toward more ethical and effective models.

Alongside my doctoral research, I teach criminal law at UCL, where I deliver seminar teaching, support student learning through problem-based approaches, and provide academic mentoring. I also supervise postgraduate dissertations and contribute to teaching on UCL’s summer programmes. My teaching emphasises clarity, structured reasoning, and the development of strong analytical and writing skills.

Prior to and alongside my academic work, I have held research and policy roles in international non-governmental organisations, including work on immigration detention, human rights, and advocacy across multiple jurisdictions. This experience informs my research, particularly in relation to the practical and institutional dimensions of detention systems and labour practices.

I am the founder of *Reform Radar*, an open-access digital platform in development that maps and analyses prison-based rehabilitation initiatives worldwide. The project aims to support comparative research, policy engagement, and evidence-based reform by centralising data across jurisdictions.

My broader research interests include criminal law, labour law, human rights, law and social justice, and emerging issues at the intersection of law and technology.
